What is the days sales in inventory?
The days sales in inventory (DSI) is the average number of days that it takes for the company to sell inventory. It helps in measuring the efficiency of sales of the company.
How to calculate days sales in inventory?
To calculate days sales in inventory you should use the following formula:
days sales in inventory = (inventory / cost of sales) * number of days
Example: In the year 2022 company x reported an ending inventory of $2,000,000 and $195,000,000 in cost of sales, calculate the days sales in inventory?
Using the days sales in inventory formula the answer will be:
days sales in inventory = (inventory / cost of sales) * number of days
days sales in inventory = ($2,000,000 / $195,000,000) * 365
days sales in inventory = 3.74 days
Why use days sales in inventory?
Analysts use days sales in inventory to measure the efficiency of sales. High sales in inventory indicates that the company has inventory that is hard to sell or the company isn't managing it's inventory in a proper way.
